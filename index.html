Just write down 1-2 questions about each talk, and submit this to me.
2) An easy entailment is propositional logic is: not (A -> B) |= A. I would like you to go through each of the equivalent conditions in the theorem we discussed on Thursday and check that they hold for these formulas (in the notation of that theorem: F_1= not (A-> B), G=A). This is one of the paradoxical implications that Graham Priest mentions in the reading on non-classical logic. In fact, he uses this implication to prove the existence of God. You might think about whether the interpretation of this entailment in terms of set-theoretic semantics shines any light on the paradoxical quality of this entailment.
